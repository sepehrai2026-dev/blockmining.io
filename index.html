Guide

How to evaluate cloud mining services

Legitimacy first

The cloud mining space has a long history of scams and Ponzi schemes. Before investing, verify that the provider has physical mining facilities you can verify, transparent hashrate reporting, and a track record of reliable payouts. If the returns sound too good to be true, they are.

Contract terms matter

Read the fine print on maintenance fees, contract duration, and termination conditions. Some contracts have clauses that automatically terminate when mining becomes unprofitable — meaning you lose your investment. Look for flexible terms and transparent fee structures.

Profitability reality check

Cloud mining is rarely more profitable than buying and holding crypto directly. The provider needs to cover hardware, electricity, cooling, staff, and profit margin — all from your contract fees. The main advantage is convenience, not returns.

Payout and withdrawal

Check minimum payout thresholds, payout frequency, and withdrawal options. The best platforms offer daily payouts to your own wallet with low minimum thresholds. Avoid platforms that lock your earnings for extended periods.
